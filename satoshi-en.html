Satoshi Nakamoto & the birth of Bitcoin

2008: global financial crisis. In the middle of the chaos, a 9-page PDF proposes another path: Bitcoin.

The Bitcoin whitepaper

The document is titled “Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System”. Satoshi describes a payment system that allows value to be transferred directly from one person to another, without a bank.

  • Purely digital currency
  • Transactions validated by a distributed network
  • Security guaranteed by cryptography
  • No central bank controlling supply

A ghost creator

Satoshi Nakamoto is a pseudonym. One person, a group, a research team? Nobody knows. We know his ideas through his emails, forum posts and code… and then he disappears.

The project, however, goes on. That’s the whole point of a truly decentralized system: it doesn’t need a guru to survive.

How does Bitcoin work?

📦 Blocks of transactions

Transactions are grouped into blocks. Each block references the previous one, forming a chain – the blockchain.

🔐 Cryptography

Digital signatures ensure that only the owner of a private key can spend the associated bitcoins.

⛏️ Mining & Proof-of-Work

Miners secure the network by solving cryptographic puzzles. In return, they receive new bitcoins as block rewards.

Key economic parameters

Parameter Value / Principle
Maximum supply 21 million bitcoins
Monetary issuance Block reward (mining)
Halving Block reward is cut in half roughly every 4 years
Decentralization No state, no central bank, open global network
