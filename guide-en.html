Practical guide: risks, security & resources

The tech is brilliant, the marketing is loud, and scams are creative. This guide is here so you don’t get wrecked on the way.

1. Extreme volatility

+30% in one week, then –50% the next month: totally normal in crypto. Basic rule: only invest money you can afford to lose without destroying your real life.

2. Scams & absurd promises

Guaranteed returns, “magic trading bots”, pyramids disguised as crypto projects… If it sounds too good to be true, it almost always is.

3. Technical risks

Lose or leak your private key and the funds are gone forever. Phishing, fake wallets, fake apps: security is not a detail, it’s the core of the whole game.

Securing your crypto: the basics

Choosing a wallet

  • Hot wallet: connected to the internet (mobile app, browser extension). Convenient for daily use, more exposed to attacks.
  • Cold wallet: offline (hardware wallet, dedicated USB device, paper). Ideal for long-term storage.

Basic good practices

  • Never share your private key or seed phrase with anyone.
  • Write down your seed phrase on paper, keep it offline.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
  • Check the destination address character by character.
  • Be suspicious of links received by email, SMS or social media.

Bitcoin economics in a nutshell

Bitcoin combines a limited supply (21 million), a predictable issuance schedule (regular halving), and a global demand that moves with adoption, speculation and trust.

  • Supply is known in advance, can’t be inflated at will.
  • No central bank to “print” more bitcoins.
  • Gradual adoption by individuals, companies, institutions.
  • Cyclical bull markets and bear markets, like any speculative asset.

Good reflex: always verify

Before following a “hot tip” or a crypto influencer, ask yourself three questions:

  • Who is speaking? (real expertise or pure marketing?)
  • What is their incentive? (commissions, affiliation, paid promo?)
  • What are the concrete risks if it goes wrong?

In crypto as everywhere else: DYOR – Do Your Own Research.
